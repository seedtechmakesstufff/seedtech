## 3. Core Philosophy & Differentiators

### 3.1 Discovery-First Sales Process
SeedTech never leads with a pitch. Every new engagement starts with an
**Assessment Walkthrough** — a 45–60 minute technical deep dive that covers:

- Users / Devices / Locations inventory
- Backup + recovery readiness
- Security posture (endpoint, MFA, access controls)
- Network, Wi-Fi, and firewall basics
- Field workflows and communications
- Vendor map (ISPs, carriers, line-of-business tools)

**Deliverable:** A short action plan with a recommended SeedCare tier and rollout path.
The goal of the first meeting is never to sign a contract — it's to schedule the
Assessment.

### 3.2 Proactive, Not Reactive
The traditional break-fix cycle:
1. Service hangs → user notices
2. User calls helpdesk → wait + triage
3. Ticket created → technician investigates → fix applied

**The SeedTech Way:**
1. Monitoring detects the issue (often before any user reports it)
2. Automated recovery scripts run (safe, pre-approved)
3. SeedTech is alerted immediately with full context
4. If auto-recovery fails, a ticket is created and escalated
5. Post-incident review to prevent recurrence

*Less disruption, faster awareness, fewer repeat issues.*

### 3.3 Shared Goals with Clients
SeedTech explicitly aligns on five values with every prospect:

| Value | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Uptime & reliability** | Systems stay running; downtime is the enemy |
| **Fast response** | Critical issues triaged in under 15 minutes |
| **Proactive prevention** | Avoid repeat issues through automation |
| **Field-first execution** | Support works in real-world conditions, not just the office |
| **Accountability + documentation** | Everything is logged, reported, and transparent |

### 3.4 No Long-Term Contracts
Every plan is **month-to-month**. SeedTech earns the business every billing cycle
instead of locking clients in with annual commitments.

### 3.5 Mix-and-Match Flexibility
Plans can be mixed and matched by role, location, or device type. Examples:

- Office staff on Plus, field crews on Essentials
- Executives on Pro, rest of team on Plus
- Per-location tiering based on criticality

Competitors force a one-size-fits-all model. SeedTech right-sizes to actual needs.

## 7. Sales Process & Engagement Model

### 7.1 First Meeting (60 Minutes)

| Block | Duration | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Welcome + Shared Goals | 5 min | Align on values — uptime, speed, prevention, accountability |
| Understanding the Client | 10 min | Map their operations: locations, office vs. field, critical apps |
| Discovery: Current IT Reality | 10 min | **The most important part.** Listen, don't sell. |
| SeedTech Support Approach | 10 min | Contrast their pain with SeedTech's stack and automation |
| Solutions & Pricing | 15 min | Present SeedCare tiers, recommend a fit, show calculator |
| Next Steps + Q&A | 10 min | Schedule the Assessment Walkthrough |

### 7.2 Discovery Questions (Key Sales Tool)
These eight questions drive the entire first meeting:

1. How are you handling IT support today? (in-house / outsourced / break-fix only?)
2. When something breaks, what happens? Who do you call? How long does it take?
3. What's been your biggest recurring tech pain point in the last 90 days?
4. Do you have proactive monitoring, or do you find out about issues when users report them?
5. Who's responsible for backups, security updates, and patching?
6. Field crews — how do they get IT support when they're on-site with a customer?
7. Do you have documentation of your network, logins, ISPs, and inventory?
8. Any current vendor frustrations? (Internet, phones, software providers?)

*Use their answers to position everything that follows. The tech stack and pricing
slides become relevant only when framed against real pain.*

### 7.3 Assessment Walkthrough (45–60 Minutes)
The low-risk, high-value next step after the first meeting:

- Review users, devices, locations
- Validate backup + recovery readiness
- Check security posture (endpoint + MFA + access)
- Assess network + Wi-Fi + firewall basics
- Map field workflows + communications
- Vendor map (ISPs, carriers, LOB tools)

**Deliverable:** Short action plan + recommended SeedCare level + rollout path.

## 9. Key Messaging for Content & SEO

### Do Say
- "Proactive IT support" — monitoring catches issues before users report them
- "Flat-rate, per-user pricing" — predictable, no surprise invoices
- "No long-term contracts" — month-to-month, earn the business every cycle
- "Unlimited remote help desk" — every plan, no ticket caps
- "Mix and match" — right-sized to actual needs, not a one-size-fits-all model
- "Field + office" — we support both environments because they have different needs
- "We handle your vendors" — ISPs, carriers, software providers, no more phone tag
- "Assessment, not a contract" — the first step is always low-risk and high-value

### Don't Say
- "Break-fix" (that's the competitor model we replace)
- "Per-device pricing" (we price per user, covering all their devices)
- "Hourly billing" (our model is the opposite)
- "Minimum seats" or "annual commitment" (we have neither)
